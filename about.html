ABOUT FSL

What is Filipino Sign Language?

Filipino Sign Language (FSL) is the national sign language of the Filipino Deaf community. It is a visual language with its own grammar and syntax, distinct from spoken Filipino and English.

Why it matters

FSL empowers the Deaf community by promoting accessibility, inclusion, and equal opportunities in education, employment, and public services. Recognizing and using FSL strengthens Deaf identity and culture in the Philippines.

FSL Law (RA 11106)

Republic Act No. 11106, also known as the FSL Law, was enacted in 2018. It declares FSL as the national sign language and mandates its use in government, education, media, and workplaces to ensure accessibility for Deaf Filipinos.
